Yearly Cost Estimate


Your costs for health care include more than just your insurance premiums.

Our Yearly Cost Estimates include not only your premiums, but also what you have to pay for costs not covered by your plan for doctors, hospital, prescriptions, and other services.

We calculate "typical" costs for someone like you so that you can easily compare plans on an estimate of total out-of-pocket costs, not just premiums.

Our cost estimates assume you use preferred providers in plans that have them.

Cost in a Bad Year


Premiums plus out-of-pocket costs in a bad year, where you have high medical expenses, can be crippling.

Our "Cost in a Bad Year" compares plans on premiums plus what you might have to pay in a bad year for doctor, hospital, drug, and other charges not fully paid for by the insurer. And it tells you how likely people like you (same age, family size, health status, and other characteristics) are to have such a high cost year.

Our cost estimates assume you use preferred providers in plans that have them.

Is my doctor in the plan?


"Is my doctor in the plan?" ranks a close second to cost considerations when you're choosing health insurance. We answer the question through an easy-to-compare, Exchange-wide doctor directory. You can see, on a single screen, which of your doctors are in which plans.

But remember, if you are choosing a plan based on doctor participation, ALWAYS call the doctor to confirm that they will be accepting new patients for the upcoming year, and to be sure they will be accepting the plan you are considering.

The maximum number of plans to compare is 12.
